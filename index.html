    WHO World Health Organization — Asia-Pacific Guidelines

    The WHO published specific BMI cutoffs for Asian populations in 2004, recognising that Asians develop metabolic risk at lower BMI levels than Western populations. This app uses the WHO Asia-Pacific thresholds: healthy BMI 18.5–22.9, overweight ≥23, obese ≥27.5. Waist circumference thresholds for Asian adults are ≥90 cm for men and ≥80 cm for women — lower than the global WHO cutoffs.

    OWID Our World in Data — Global Obesity & NCD Trends

    Our World in Data aggregates long-run health statistics from the Global Burden of Disease study and WHO. The population context figures used in this app — including Malaysia's overweight prevalence (~50% of adults) and the tripling of obesity rates since 1990 — are drawn from OWID's published country-level data for Malaysia.

    OWID obesity data →
    NHMS National Health & Morbidity Survey 2019 — Malaysia

    The NHMS is Malaysia's most comprehensive national health survey, conducted by the Institute for Public Health under the Ministry of Health Malaysia. The 2019 edition surveyed over 10,000 adults and reported national prevalence of overweight, obesity, abdominal obesity, hypertension, and diabetes. The Malaysian population distribution shown in this app's chart uses NHMS 2019 mean BMI and waist circumference figures by sex as the basis for the simulated reference cloud.
